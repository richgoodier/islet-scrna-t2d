Final project overview

The final group project is a reproducibility study. Each group is tasked with finding a current research paper (written in the last five (5) years). The groups will then identify a pipeline—or portion of one—and attempt to reproduce the results using what they have learned throughout the course.
Second sub-component

The individual write-up must include:

        Introduction/background
        Statement of need: Why do they believe the paper was written in the first place?
        Identification of the original researcher's hypothesis.
        Identification of any substantiating or corroborating information the original authors use to support their findings.
        A statement of the validity of the methods and results.
        Identification of potential drawbacks or oversights the original authors "missed."
            Must be uniquely different than any identified by the original authors
        Citations to corroborate their opinions.

Both the in-line and Work Cited citations must be in a consistent format throughout the paper.
